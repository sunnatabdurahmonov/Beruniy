In Urganj, Beruni dealt with mathematics, astronomy, as well as some problems of physics and mineralogy. It was here that the idea of ​​using specific gravities to identify minerals and systematize them was born.

In the summer of 1017, by order of the Turkish king Mahmud Ghaznavi, Beruni was taken to Ghazna as a prisoner. He lived there in difficult conditions. After 1019, he reached the condition of doing scientific work. In 1022-1024 Mahmud took Beruni with him during his march to India. Even during the trip, Beruni studied science. He measured the length of one degree of the meridian of the globe near the fort of Nandna in Punjab, and it was 110,895 km. found out that If this information is compared with the result of modern measurements - 111.1 km, it can be seen how accurate Beruni's measurements are. He spent most of his life in Ghazna, then the capital of the Ghaznavid state in present-day southeastern Afghanistan. While traveling to the Indian subcontinent, he wrote a treatise on Indian culture called Tarikh al-Hind (History of India) in 1030 after studying the Hindu religion practiced in India. He was a remarkably unbiased writer for his time. The customs and beliefs of various peoples, and his scientific objectivity earned him the title of al-Ustad ("The Teacher") for his excellent portrayal of India in the early 11th century.

Great scientists such as Pythagoras, Aristotle, Archimedes, Eratosthenes, who passed before Christ, expressed the opinion that the Earth is round and spherical. For example, in 250 BC, the Alexandrian scientist Eratosthenes said that the Earth is spherical and determined its dimensions as follows. He measured the distance between the cities of Alexandria and Sienia (now Aswan) by the duration of the caravans, then divided this value by the difference of the latitudes of the two cities and calculated the radius of the Earth as 6,840 kilometers. The length of the meridian arc is 119,444 degrees, or the length of the meridian arc is 70,121, and he calculated that this arc is 1/50 of the length of the meridian circle. He came to the opinion that the length of the Earth's meridian circle is about 39,500 kilometers. After Eratosthenes, a number of Egyptian, Chinese and Greek scientists determined the size of the Earth using Eratosthenes' method. In the Middle Ages, scientists from our country made great contributions in determining the size of the Earth.
